Objective:

To determine the concentration of an unknown acid solution by titrating it with a standardized base solution.

Materials:

  • Burette
  • Pipette
  • Erlenmeyer flask
  • White tile (for better visibility of the color change)
  • Standard solution of Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH), 0.1 M
  • Unknown solution of Hydrochloric Acid (HCl)
  • Phenolphthalein indicator
  • Distilled water

Procedure:

  1. Preparation:
    • Rinse the burette with the NaOH solution and then fill it with NaOH. Make sure there are no air bubbles in the tip of the burette.
    • Use the pipette to transfer 25.0 mL of the unknown HCl solution into an Erlenmeyer flask.
    • Add 2-3 drops of phenolphthalein indicator to the HCl solution. The solution should be colorless.
  2. Titration:
    • Place the Erlenmeyer flask on a white tile to better observe the color change.
    • Slowly add the NaOH solution from the burette to the HCl solution, while swirling the flask continuously.
    • Continue adding the base until you observe a faint pink color that persists for at least 30 seconds. This indicates that the endpoint has been reached.
  3. Recording Data:
    • Record the initial and final readings of the burette to determine the volume of NaOH used.
    • Repeat the titration at least two more times to ensure accuracy and consistency in your results.

Calculations:

Use the formula:

M1V1 = M2V2

Where:

  • M1 = Molarity of HCl (unknown)
  • V1 = Volume of HCl (25.0 mL)
  • M2 = Molarity of NaOH (0.1 M)
  • V2 = Volume of NaOH used (from burette reading)

Calculate the concentration of the unknown HCl solution using the average volume of NaOH from your trials.

Safety Precautions:

  • Wear safety goggles and gloves throughout the experiment.
  • Handle acids and bases with care to avoid skin irritation.
  • Dispose of all chemicals as instructed by your teacher.

Questions for Students:

  1. Why is phenolphthalein used as an indicator in this experiment?
  2. What could be some sources of error in this titration?
  3. How would the results be affected if the endpoint was overshot?

Conclusion:

Summarize the findings, including the concentration of the HCl solution and any potential sources of error.

Conclusion Questions:

  1. What was the average concentration of the HCl solution from your trials?
  2. How consistent were your titration results across the different trials?
  3. What might cause differences in your measured volumes of NaOH between trials?
  4. How could you improve the accuracy of your titration experiment?
  5. What role did the indicator play in helping you determine the endpoint of the titration?
